Theory by a Young Girl

9 March: Tiqqun 1

Of course the young girl is a gendered figure, or she would have zero potency.

Saya makes herself a human through gender first.
The Young-Girl is optimistic, thrilled, positive, happy, enthusiastic, joyful; in other words, she suffers. The Young-Girl is produced wherever nihilism begins to speak of happiness. There is nothing special about the Young-Girl; that is her ‘beauty.’ The Young-Girl is an optical illusion. From afar, she is an angel, and up close, she is a beast. The Young-Girl doesn’t age. She decomposes.
– Tiqqun, Preliminary Materials for a Theory of the Young-Girl: 45

The theory of the young girl is nonsense in both of Wittgenstein’s senses. For it doesn’t refer to anything existing, and it twists the sense of ordinary language beyond anything useful. It is another clear illustration of philosophical excess, language gone on holiday, an engine left running past its use.

When the Young-Girl gives in to her own insignificance, she still manages to find glory in it, because she is ‘having fun.’ (36)

It reads like a misogynist screed in a feminist coat, a 1990s equivalent of Marx’s ‘Jewish Question’ in its misplaced use of hate-language on a macrocosmic scale which never quite eludes its reactionary character. Tired stereotypes of the superficial, petty, consumerist adolescent are regurgitated, as are claims of societal feminization and male subjugation. A revenge fantasy. Nothing inherently wrong with that, but it makes for bad theory.

The Young-Girl doesn’t kiss you, she drools over you through her teeth. Materialism of secretions. (39)

Incel ressentiment is most clearly apparent in the claim that young girls can never be possessed, and always be withdrawn. Here the young girl’s objectivity is abjectly reified as a demand for intimacy qua sexuality. This is not parody; it is genuine hatred. Behind the lofty condemnations of seduction, exchange, duplicity – lies simple misogyny.

Love for the Young-Girl is just autism for two. (30)

Asserting that the state and capitalist relations have so thoroughly saturated society, social relations, individuals, affects, desires and pleasures, that we see a narrowing of social horizons on an unprecedented scale for the capitalist age – is of course nothing new. Jackson, Debord, Fisher – and so on. Moreover, it is doable without abstraction. Ontologizing consumption accomplishes nothing but smug self-satisfaction.

The Young-Girl loves her illusions. […] The Young-Girl knows everything as devoid of consequences. (38)

What above all stands out is how alien the equivalency of youth and girlhood with ‘self-valorization’ is. It would seem a trans woman is the most alienated being there is! And in a sense it is so. But not as Tiqqun outline it. Their contempt is crystal clear from the condemnation of autism, of ‘erratic’ or ‘abnormal’ intimacy, of monstrosity, of nihilism, of solipsism OR psychosis, delusion, insignificance and, above all it seems, the negation of time.

The Young-Girl […] is the negation of the passage of time, and the obscuring of human finitude. (33)

In these traits we can indeed see the shape of a young girl, but a young girl of another kind, a young girl as lived, magical, enchanted subjectivity. The young girl – in the actual sense – who sees her homology with civilization and God alike, the properly nihilist young girl who embodies the Real and thus both rejects and loves the world, the figure of the tragic, the abused, the playful, the open secret who embodies the phallus, jouissance.

The Young-Girl resembles her photo. (33)

She may be a product, a commodity; she is sold to us, she is an image. But she herself is – clearly – alienated from all of this. She is far removed from the complacent consumer; she is what is to be consumed, and like any commodity – indeed, like any piece of meat – she is discarded, disposed of, expelled.

Despair and hopelessness marks the quality of dysphoria, burning the border between the world and impossibility deep into me, making its omnipresence unbearably visible.
‘Dysphoria Means Total Destroy’

The young girl is visible everywhere, but she is suspect; innocent in nature, depraved in function. Socially, of course. She herself is naught but herself, in all its artificiality. It’s empty, yes, of course; “the non-being she is”;(43) “to be a Young-Girl is to be nothing.”(34) But we are all nothing. That is the truth revealed by the young girl. That is why she is outside time, which is nothing but progress.

Civilization is progress. Civilization is time.

“The Young-Girl never creates anything; all in all, she only recreates herself.”(24) Indeed.

Saya, like the dandelion, recreates herself; this is her power. She makes all the world her. The truth in the notion that we must rid ourselves of externals lies in this: that we must make everything us. It means: exorcise the phantom that is objectivity with the light of subjectivity. It all must be us – and not us, the all. This is the distinction between willing the world and simple fatalism.

14 February: Meaning

Schizophrenia is a truth, a deep truth; a truth which society tacitly embraces but consciously rejects. Another open secret; an irreducible real which is always there.

Schizophrenia is an opening, expansion of experience/perception (not just ‘the mind’) to new potentialities in the infinite vastness that is God. It is the cosmic rooftop battle above the descent into the world of facts.
In plunging into the unknown, we enter the waters of unintelligibility.
– Alyson Escalante, ‘Gender Nihilism’

Most experience need not be filtered through meaning. 

Schematically, meaning is third-order. God —> experience —> meaning. Experience vacillates between the first and third orders…

All categories are truly, profoundly fake. ‘Pragmatism’ smothers this insight in the grave. Accepting the world is accepting civilization; it is civilizing oneself. There always has to be an element of refusal that breaks the regime of implications, the metaphysics of causality.

Causality is a heuristic, a schema.
A rejection of meaning, a rejection of known possibility, a rejection of being itself. Nihilism. That is our stance and method. (Escalante)

Gender, sexuality, nations, states, race, mental illness, nature, cities, cars, class, capital, schools, parliament, militaries, laws, police – are fake, abstractions that we reject; this is why we must be anarchist, why we must be a little utopian; utopian in spirit, in will, in imaginary, in magic, in becoming. We can be whatever we want to be.

– George Jackson, Blood in My Eye: 102
Categories of meaning are totalitarian; but there are also categories of experience, phenomena as they are experienced, perceived, not interpreted. These are inherently fluid, unstable. These are the phenomena of dialectics.

Bare matter is a delusion. The ‘higher’ and the ‘lower’ are the same.

Nietzsche’s nihilist ‘beyond’ is here. It is immanent.

It’s all the same. That is why there is difference.

‘The world’ is on a continuum with our perception of it; hence worlds are made and unmade as easy as breathing. Our dreams are as real as our waking perception. I can summon anything, anyone. I am all-powerful. I can be anything, I can do anything, I can get anything.

16 March: Civilization

Porcupine Tree and Sakai each try to grapple with the ‘existential malaise’ that comes from the inherently schizophrenic character of capitalism. Wilson is a typical ‘objectivist’ or realist liberal, who attributes it to objects, clearly identifiable things – screens and medication. Sakai gets closer to the truth in emphasizing ‘material’ factors – neocolonialism, declassing, etc. But even this is too ‘objectivist’ in a sense. The point is precisely subjective experience, i.e. phenomena. Subjects in dialectic with ‘objective’ factors, be they material or ‘material.’

The communist notion of ‘the material’ is a very curious abstraction. Class and modes of production are hardly ‘material,’ after all. The terminology betrays a continued attachment to ‘objectivism’ – when the force of dialectics is that it is magic.

The power-discursive-phenomenal network which I call ‘everyday life’ is one massive magic spell, an illusion cast over another, more profound magical network: the underlying schizophrenic structure which the civilized would deny out of existence were it not for its unbearable reality. This is how they can delude themselves that everything is rational, ‘normal.’ Capitalism as a ‘material reality’ is characterized by these two ‘levels.’ Neither is ‘more’ nor ‘less’ capitalist. Every mode of production appropriates OR produces its apollonian and dionysiac aspects.

The crucial distinction from other modes of production is that they accepted the magical as their corresponding ‘everyday life.’ Capitalism ‘dispels illusions’ but cannot erase schizophrenia. This explains its particular character (i.e. capitalist schizophrenia).

The mediocrity of our age – and every ‘civilized’ age is mediocre – is not to be lamented; it is a call for us to seize the moment, to hammer out something great, to prove our worth, to revolutionize the world–

沙耶の唄
Holes in the world are everywhere, even in the midst of civilization.
One only needs to look–

Can you not hear the wails of endless ages past, being swept away mercilessly by the raging torrent down into the abyss of oblivion? This is what it means to be alive – to build one’s house on tragedy upon tragedy, sedimented and ossified, crushed and forgotten, yet always, inexorably there. This is what the Demon challenges us to repeat, this is what it demands we love. If you can see clearly, then look!

沙耶の唄
Once you have glimpsed even the tiniest fraction of the world’s true face, you have no choice but to watch in horror as the veil rolls itself back inch by inch.

We must all adopt a tragic view of the world, shorn of all reactionary pretense, of all ‘patriarchal drivel.’ For the world is an ever-unfolding tragedy – and that is where its beauty lies.

The tragic view is not ‘life is suffering,’ but an affirmation of suffering as a part, an inevitable part of life. It is the catharsis of tragedy amplified to the scale of the world.

There is a ‘higher’ sadness; it is tragedy.

And here is the problem with the figure of the redeemer: he does not acknowledge the element of suffering in life. He simply affirms, never denies, and in so doing, in that very practice, denies a core part of all life. The sage, the unending ‘flow state,’ nirvana – is a lie. There are only demons.

Using Goethe’s terminology, we could characterize this dimension of love, desire, and the irrational as the ‘demonic.’ […] It is a force which gives its holders an incredible power over beings and things. The demonic represents a kind of natural magic within the dimension of the irrational and inexplicable. This irrational element is the motor force indispensable for all creation; it is the blind, inexorable dynamic which we cannot escape, but must learn how to use.
– Pierre Hadot, ‘The Figure of Socrates’: 164.

We cannot always love. That is why we must become tragic.

‘What time is it right now?’

The Norse did not perceive any of this as a matter of cause and effect; instead the events were all coextensive, simply happening; the past, the present and the future all folded in on each other. I.e.: time is spherical, time made space. Like the pages of a book. 

This is the truth grasped in the plateau structure of Capitalism and Schizophrenia vol. 2.

These notes themselves have no linearity, are constantly folding back in on themselves, pointing towards something other while operating in the omnipresent other worlds of earlier days. Things are not always written the day they are thought or articulated, but appear as flashes in convergence of a grasped past, a timeless now and a glimpsed future: they are well and truly coextensive.

The true picture of the past flits by. The past can be seized only as an image which flashes up at the insant when it can be recognized and is never seen again. 
Walter Benjamin, ‘On the Concept of History’: 255

This is what mythic time is; the precise order does not matter, is bent to the speaker’s needs at a given ‘moment of crisis.’ It differs from cyclical time in that it does not return, for what could be returned is already there. It is closer to messianic time: the notion that from the conditions before us emerges a possibility of eternity in redemption; revolution not as the consummation, but the disruption of progress OR linear time; prehistory come to a close.

No fact that is a cause is for that very reason historical. It became historical posthumously, as it were, through events that may be separated from it by thousands of years. A historian who takes this as his point of departure stops telling the sequence of events like the beads of a rosary. Instead, he grasps the constellation which his era has formed with a definite earlier one. (263)
This formula reveals a profound insight that history is nonlinear, folded in on itself. Not points on a line but constellations (stars whose lights still shine may be dead, and stars may yet be born).
History is the subject of a structure whose site is not homogeneous, empty time, but time filled by the presence of the now. (261)
Sleep must similarly be regarded as neither homogeneous nor empty, but as a magical sphere of its own. It is not death.
Dreams are also the past. Worlds omnipresent but only grasped in a flash. Dreams are other realities.

Life is not experienced as a line but as an immanent sphere without time. It is the tyranny of the clock that imports it to the now. Time only exists in retrospect and prophecy.

A historical materialist cannot do without the notion of a present which is not a transition, but in which time stands still and has come to a stop. (262)

The distinction between mythic and historical time is the notion of causality. But the present remains timeless.

In either case, the future only adds more layers; more heaps on the rubble pile.

All of history is tragic, not just civilization.

Everyday life tends towards timelessness but can’t stabilize in it because capital keeps pushing it along. It embodies a false timelessness. The same has been true of other civilizational forms, but through their capture of mythic, cyclical or messianic time rather than linear time OR progress.

Linear time grows out of messianic time.

Lines appear as a flash and are then lost forever – tragic. But they cannot be recaptured; thus: waiting.

Another flash will appear. Messianic time.
There is a secret agreement between past generations and the present one. Our coming was expected on earth. (254)
